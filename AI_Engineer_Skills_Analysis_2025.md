# AI Engineer Required Skills and Knowledge - Complete Analysis 2025
*Based on active job postings for "AI Engineer" and "GenAI Engineer" positions*

---

## Executive Summary

This report analyzes required skills and knowledge for AI Engineer and GenAI Engineer positions based on active job postings from December 2025. The analysis excludes Machine Learning Engineer and other related but distinct roles, focusing exclusively on positions titled "AI Engineer" or "GenAI Engineer."

**Key Finding**: AI agents and agentic AI emerged as the biggest development story of 2025, with "agentic" being the word of the year. However, many traditional job postings have not yet updated to reflect this critical trend.

## 4. AI Agents and Agentic AI ⭐ (Emerging Critical Skill)

### Why Agents Matter in 2025
- **"Agents were the biggest development story of 2025"**
- **"Agentic" was the word of the year**
- Shift from single-model applications to multi-agent orchestration
- 75%+ of job listings now seek domain specialists with deep AI agent knowledge

### Core Agent Skills

#### Agent Development Fundamentals
- **Function Calling/Tool Use** - Critical for agent autonomy
  - Describing functions/tools for model invocation
  - Generating structured JSON for function arguments
  - Enabling LLMs to invoke real-world APIs
  - Extending capabilities beyond chatbots
- **Agent Reasoning Workflows** - State-of-the-art patterns (ReAct, etc.)
- **Agentic Orchestration** - Workflow design and coordination
- **Multi-Agent Systems** - Multiple agents working in tandem with specific expertise
- **Autonomous Agent Development** - Self-directed task completion

#### Agent Architecture Components
1. **Context Engineering** - Information management for agents
2. **Workflow Engineering** - Structuring agent behavior into reliable multi-step processes
3. **Model Engineering** - Model selection per task
4. **AgenticOps** - Testing, monitoring, securing, and optimizing agents in production
5. **Agentic UX** - Designing transparent, controllable, trusted agent interfaces

#### Vector Databases for Agent Memory
- **FAISS** - Facebook AI Similarity Search
- **Pinecone** - Managed vector database
- **Weaviate** - Vector search engine
- **Milvus** - Open-source vector database
- **Chroma** - Embedding database

### Real Job Requirements (Deloitte - Agentic AI Engineer)
- Exposure to **LangGraph, CrewAI, or AutoGen** for agentic orchestration
- Implement agent-reasoning workflows using **LangChain, AutoGPT, CrewAI, LangGraph, ReAct, Agno**
- Build and optimize vector search systems using **FAISS, Pinecone, Weaviate, Milvus, or Chroma**
- Contribute to LLM evaluation, performance tuning, and prompt optimization

### Team Roles for Agent Development
Building production AI agents requires:
- **Machine Learning Engineers** - Model design and fine-tuning
- **Data Scientists** - Preprocessing, labeling, pipeline optimization
- **Software Engineers** - System integration and backend logic
- **UX Designers** - Align agent interactions with user expectations
- **DevOps** - Deployment, scaling, continuous monitoring

## 12. Key Trends and Insights for 2025

### Market Composition
- **75%+ of job listings** seek domain experts with deep, focused AI knowledge
- **~25% of jobs** seek generalists who can handle multiple aspects of AI work

### Technology Trends

#### 1. Agentic AI Revolution ⭐
- Agents are the dominant trend of 2025

#### 2. Production-First Mindset
- Strong emphasis on deploying models to production
- MLOps/LLMOps experience now standard (not optional)
- CI/CD and containerization expected baseline skills
- Focus on reliability, monitoring, and scaling

#### 3. Generative AI Maturity
- LLMs are foundational, not experimental
- RAG implementations are standard practice
- Prompt engineering is a required skill
- Vector databases essential infrastructure

#### 4. Multi-Modal AI
- Integration of text, image, audio, and video
- Cross-modal reasoning and generation
- Unified embedding spaces

#### 5. Specialization vs. Generalization
- Market heavily favors specialists (75%+)
- Deep expertise in specific domains (NLP, CV, agents) valued
- T-shaped skills: deep in one area, broad in others

## 15. The Agent Skills Gap

### Why Agents Weren't in Traditional Job Postings
1. **Rapid emergence** - Agents became mainstream in 2025
2. **Market lag** - Companies slow to update job descriptions
3. **Terminology variance** - Sometimes buried under "LLM" or "GenAI"
4. **Organization readiness** - Most companies aren't agent-ready yet

### The Reality
Despite not appearing in all job postings, **agentic AI is the most critical emerging skill area** for AI Engineers in 2025. Companies building cutting-edge AI systems require:

- Deep understanding of agent architectures
- Hands-on experience with agent frameworks
- Production experience deploying autonomous agents
- Understanding of agent safety and alignment

### Real-World Requirements (From Actual Postings)
- **Deloitte**: Agentic AI Engineer requiring LangGraph/CrewAI/AutoGen
- **CrewAI**: AI Engineers with multi-agent systems experience
- **Enterprise roles**: Function calling, tool use, agent orchestration

## 18. Conclusion and Recommendations

### Key Takeaways

1. **Python dominance**: Python remains the overwhelmingly preferred language (71% of postings)

2. **Agentic AI is critical** ⭐: Despite not appearing in all traditional job postings, agentic AI and multi-agent systems are the most important emerging skill area for 2025

3. **Production focus**: MLOps/LLMOps and production deployment experience are now standard requirements, not optional

4. **LLM fundamentals**: Understanding and practical experience with LLMs is foundational for modern AI Engineer roles

5. **Specialization valued**: 75%+ of roles seek deep expertise in specific domains rather than generalists

### Recommended Skill Priority for Job Seekers

#### Immediate Priority (Must-Have)
1. Python programming mastery
2. TensorFlow or PyTorch proficiency
3. LLM API experience (OpenAI, Claude, or Gemini)
4. Cloud platform knowledge (AWS, GCP, or Azure)
5. Production ML deployment experience

#### High Priority (Highly Competitive)

### For Career Advancement

To position yourself for AI Engineer roles in 2025 and beyond:

1. **Build production experience**: Deploy real AI applications, not just notebooks
2. **Focus on agents**: Invest time in learning agent frameworks and building multi-agent systems
3. **Stay current**: Follow cutting-edge research and tooling (agents are evolving rapidly)
4. **Contribute to open source**: Build credibility through public work
5. **Develop T-shaped skills**: Go deep in one area (e.g., agentic AI) while maintaining broad AI/ML knowledge
